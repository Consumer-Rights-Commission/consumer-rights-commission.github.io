Resolution 001

On Manufacturer Restrictions to Independent Repair

Under Member Review 2026 · ← Back to Resolutions
Status Under Member Review
Submitted 2026
Topic Right to Repair
Scope Global
Related Article Why Manufacturers Don't Want You Fixing Your Own Device

Summary

Evidence indicates that hardware manufacturers' warranty policies and parts restrictions result in measurable harm to consumers through increased costs and reduced product longevity. The Commission recommends members consider supporting Right to Repair legislation in their respective jurisdictions.

Findings

The Commission finds that manufacturer-imposed repair restrictions, including parts pairing systems, proprietary diagnostic tools, and warranty terms that void coverage upon third-party repair, systematically reduce consumer access to affordable repair options.

These restrictions demonstrably increase the total cost of device ownership and contribute to accelerated device replacement cycles, producing both economic harm to consumers and measurable environmental impact through increased electronic waste.

Legislative efforts in multiple jurisdictions have begun to address these restrictions. The Commission finds that consumer and legislative pressure has produced documented changes in manufacturer behavior, indicating that coordinated consumer action is effective in this area.

Recommendation

The Consumer Rights Commission recommends that members consider the following actions:

  • Contact elected representatives in support of Right to Repair legislation where applicable
  • Prioritize purchasing devices with documented repairability ratings
  • Support independent repair businesses as an alternative to manufacturer-authorized repair
  • Consider the availability of repair options as a factor in purchasing decisions

Company Response

No response received. This resolution is currently under member review.
